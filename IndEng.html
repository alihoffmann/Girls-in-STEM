What is Industrial Engineering?

industrial engineering (n): engineering applied to the planning, design, and control of industrial operations src: dictionary.com




But what does this mean?

Industrial engineers work in factories and help to design factories and processes which minimize waste. They attempt to eliminate wastefulness in the production of materials. They create systems which utilize workers, machines, materials, information, and energy to create this product.

Let's break that down a bit further...

Industrial engineers work in many different industries. Their primary goal is to reduce waste. By reducing waste, they can create less harm to the environment, maximize profits, and decrease the cost of products for consumers. Industrial engineers use data and experiences to determine how to safely and efficiently manipulate production.

Industrial engineers are useful during the process of designing of a factory and remodeling the factory. They are continuously working on projects to help maximize a process or factory. With each job, there are constantly new problems to solve and new materials to order. It is imperative that industrial engineers, like most engineers, work well in groups because they work in teams to design processes.

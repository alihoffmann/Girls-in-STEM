What is Chemical Engineering?

chemical engineering (n): the branch of engineering concerned with the design, operation, maintenance, and manufacture of the plant and machinery used in industrial chemical processes src: dictionary.com


But what does this mean?




Chemical engineers apply the principles of chemistry, biology, physics, and math to solve problems that involve the production or use of chemicals, fuel, drugs, food, and many other products. They design processes and equipment for large-scale manufacturing, plan and test production methods and byproducts treatment, and direct facility operations.

Let's break that down a bit further...

Chemical Engineers have a broad knowledge of chemistry, math, physics, and biology. Because they have so much knowledge in these subjects, they are able to apply this to industries to solve problems. They work in industries which use any form of chemicals (so, basically almost every industry).

Because chemical engineers find themselves working in various fields and areas, they have a wide knowledge of not only chemicals and chemical processes, but of other engineering fields. Typically, chemical engineers work to design processes and equipment for manufacturing and testing methods.

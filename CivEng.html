What is Civil Engineering?

civil engineering (n): The branch of engineering that specializes in the design and construction of structures such as bridges, roads, and dams. src: dictionary.com


But what does this mean?




Civil engineers conceive, design, build, supervise, operate, construct, and maintain infrastructure projects and systems in the public and private sector, including roads, buildings, airports, tunnels, dams, bridges, and systems for water supply and sewage treatment.

Let's break that down a bit further...

Civil Engineers work on large scale projects, to say the least. They work on projects ranging from bridges to tunnels to major complexes. They also work on infrastructure and roads. Civil Engineers can be thought of as architects who work on larger projects.

Civil Engineers are important for designing structural pieces and infrastructure, and they ensure that current designs will satisfy the needs of the project. Essentially, Civil Engineers work on designing large scale projects which somehow beenfit the public. The scope of their field is not just designing bridges and tunnels, but it does expand to water supply and how to transfer water to and from different places.

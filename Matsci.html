What is Materials Science and Engineering?

Materials Science (n): the study of the characteristics and uses of various materials, as glass, plastics, and metals. src: dictionary.com




But what does this mean?

To begin, materials science and materials engineering are essentially the same field. In the field, engineers work with the design and application of various materials. Materials scientsts or engineers research and study the structures and properties of various types of materials in order to strengthen the materials or to determine the best way to develop new materials with certain properties. They develop, process, and test materials used to create various products.

Let's break that down a bit further...

Materials scientists work on developing new materials. These materials can be used for many different products. Maybe it's a biodegradable plastic or a material which can be used in a replacement hip which is stronger and less expensive to produce. Either way, materials scientists develop all of the man-made materials which are used in our everyday life.

Materials scientists use their knowledge of physics, chemistry, and biology to create the materials. Material scientists utilize both the scientific method and the engineering cycle (the process engineers use to brainstorm, protoytpe, test, and finalize products). The combination of these methods helps engineers and scientists to create successful products which can be beneficial to a community.

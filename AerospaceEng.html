What is Aerospace Engineering?

aerospace engineering (n): the branch of engineering that deals with the design, development, testing, and production of aircraft and related systems (aeronautical engineering) and of spacecraft, missiles, rocket-propulsion systems, and other equipment operating beyond the earth's atmosphere (astronautical engineering) src: dictionary.com


But what does this mean?




Aerospace Engineers work to design and improve aeornautical systems. They use their knowledge of physics and math to develop solutions to problems and to make innovations.

Let's break that down a bit further...

Aerospace Engineers work with airplanes, rockets, and on topics related to flight and aerodynamics. They work to develop answers to current issues and to improve current technology. Aerospace Engineers work with other types of engineers to create the best product.

They mainly design aircraft, spacecraft, satellites, and missiles, but there are other options. Like other engineers, Aerospace Engineers must test prototypes to ensure that their designs function correctly, and these prototypes are used to trouble shoot design issues. It is important that there are no major flaws in the final product because these issues can kill people.

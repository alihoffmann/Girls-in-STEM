What is Information Security?

Information Security (n): (infosec) is a set of strategies for managing the processes, tools and policies necessary to prevent, detect, document and counter threats to digital and non-digital information. Infosec responsibilities include establishing a set of business processes that will protect information assets regardless of how the information is formatted or whether it is in transit, is being processed or is at rest in storage. src: searchsecurity.com




But what does this mean?

The primary goal of individuals who work in information security is to ensure the security of information and prevent it from losing its reliatbility. Information security analysts work based off of the following principles: confidentiality, intregrity, and availability. src: searchsecurity.comv

Let's break that down a bit further...

To begin, let's explain what the three pinciples mean and how they work. Confidentiality is the ability to ensure that private information is only released to authorized groups. Integrity is related to preventing unauthorized groups from changing data. Availability means that the authorized groups are able to access data when they need it. These principles are the foundation of information security. When one of these aspects is violated, it can create issues for the company, and it can violate other principles.

Information security is closely related to cyber security, or the name given to keeping information safe online. Information security can relate to anything regarding how safe information is. As the world becomes more dependent on technology, it is more important to protect the information and to keep people and companies' information secure.
